Not every recommendation is one you want to act on immediately. **Defer** parks it; **Save as current thesis** marks it as your working baseline.

## Save as current thesis

When the engine produces a view you agree with and want to track against, save it as your current thesis. Comparisons elsewhere in the workspace then show drift from this thesis.

1. Open the [Decision page](/help/reference/pages/decision) for the recommendation.
2. Click **Save as current thesis** in the action bar.
3. Optional: add a one-line reason for the audit trail.

The thesis is one per user at a time. Saving a new thesis replaces the old one; the prior thesis remains in the audit trail.

## Defer a decision

When a recommendation is reasonable but you do not want it to auto-promote on the next cycle (a regime transition is underway, you are on vacation, you want a manual review), defer it.

1. Open the [Decision page](/help/reference/pages/decision) for the recommendation.
2. Click **Defer decision** in the action bar.
3. Add a reason — recommended for audit clarity.

A deferred recommendation will not auto-promote even if upstream policy would have allowed it. The next cycle is unaffected.

## Difference between the two

| | Save as current thesis | Defer decision |
|---|---|---|
| **Effect on the queue** | Recommendation remains active and may auto-promote. | Recommendation is held; no auto-promotion. |
| **Effect on comparisons** | Other engines / cycles show drift from this thesis. | None. |
| **Reversibility** | Save again to replace; no explicit "unsave." | Click **Un-defer** in the queue to restore. |
