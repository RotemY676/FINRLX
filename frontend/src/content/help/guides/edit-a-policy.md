Policies are the soft constraints applied on top of every recommendation. They differ from hard constraints in that you can change them between cycles. The most common edits are raising the cash floor when the regime turns risk-off and tightening the confidence floor when data quality is in question.

Every policy edit is versioned. The next recommendation cycle picks up the new values automatically.
