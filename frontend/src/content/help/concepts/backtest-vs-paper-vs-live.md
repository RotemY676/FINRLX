A strong backtest is not a promise of strong live returns. Paper trading sits between them and exposes the gap. The three differ along three dimensions: data quality (point-in-time vs. survivorship-cleaned), execution realism (assumed fills vs. broker fills with partial executions and slippage), and regime coverage (one historical window vs. the live regime).

This page lays out how FINRLX runs each, what to compare across the three, and how much of the gap is "normal."
