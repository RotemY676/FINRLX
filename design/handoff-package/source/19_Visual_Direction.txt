This document proposes the visual direction for QuantPipeline. It is not a frozen style guide but a constrained direction that should guide exploration and keep future screen work coherent.

# 1. Visual intent
The platform should feel credible, analytical, and calm. It should avoid both consumer-finance gamification and institutional terminal intimidation. The desired tone is high-trust, modern, and quietly sophisticated.
Use clarity and contrast rather than ornamental intensity.
Promote disciplined reading order through spacing and hierarchy.
Reserve strong color for actions, risk states, and meaningful changes.
Make dense data feel organized rather than crowded.

# 3. Typography and rhythm
Display typography is reserved for page titles and major metrics only.
Section headings should be compact and unambiguous.
Body text should stay short, especially in evidence narratives and methodology notes.
Meta labels should be quiet but readable, never microscopic.

# 5. Charting direction
Charts should explain the recommendation, not compete with it. The default chart posture is direct labeling, minimal clutter, and explicit overlays for events, confidence ranges, and scenario changes.
Prefer a small number of highly legible charts to many ornamental mini-charts.
Use event markers and annotations to tie visible movements to evidence.
When showing confidence or uncertainty, favor bands or intervals that are visually distinct but restrained.
Legends should be minimized; direct labels or inline labels are preferred.

# 6. Motion and interaction styling
Motion should reinforce structure: panel expansion, sheet emergence, state confirmation.
Typical duration range should be fast and calm, roughly 150 to 220 milliseconds for most UI transitions.
Hover, focus, disabled, and pressed states must be visually consistent across all controls.
Reduced-motion accessibility support is mandatory from the start.

# 7. Iconography and illustration
Use a simple line-icon family with consistent stroke weight.
Icons help scanning but never replace textual meaning for high-stakes actions.
Illustration should be reserved for empty states and guidance moments, not for analytical surfaces.

# 11. Brand expression and anti-goals
The product should not imitate meme-stock, gaming, or casino-like financial apps.
The product should not look like an engineering prototype held together by generic admin templates.
A restrained premium tone is preferred over loud futurism.
Brand presence should appear through consistency, confidence, and exactness rather than decoration.

# 12. Handoff guidance for future visual exploration
Explore at least two density levels for desktop before locking the final system.
Validate chart legibility in grayscale and reduced-width layouts early.
Create paired light and dark examples for one decision screen and one ops screen before scaling the full library.
